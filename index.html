Education

University of Huddersfield, PhD in Metasurfaces for optical metrology

I am currently in my write-up period for my PhD at the University of Huddersfield, where I have been working on the design, simulation and experimentation of metasurfaces. My project focus has been on using metasurfaces to shrink the size of chromatic confocal instruments, in particular, creating compact sensing probes and systems for on-machine and in-situ metrology, aiming to challenge the status quo and progress towards Indsutry 4.0 and creating seamless automated manufacturing, prioritising traceability of errors, minimising time-consumption and introducing the ability to diagnose and correct manufacturing errors there and then.

University of Leeds, MEng, BEng, Mechanical Engineering with year in industry

I acquired my integrated masters in mechanical engineering at the University of Leeds, where various projects has been taken, including robotics related projects in my third and final year. A year in industry at Malvern Panalytical was also taken place as the fourth year within the degree. My placement year exposed me to optical instrumentation, which later lead me to the path of my PhD and my current passion and career direction.
